# Veriq Business and Adoption Model

## Customer, user and problem

The paying customer is expected to be a Zimbabwean secondary school, responsible authority, school group or education programme. The day-to-day users are heads, deputy heads, heads of department and authorised student-support staff.

Schools already collect attendance, behaviour and internal-assessment data, but the records are often reviewed separately and after a problem becomes visible. Veriq combines the three sources into traceable early patterns, gives leaders a whole-school view and lets them ask Beacon for a grounded explanation before assigning a human-owned action.

Veriq does not sell an official pass-rate prediction and does not replace ZIMSEC, the school information system or educator judgement. Its value is earlier interpretation and a more consistent evidence-to-action workflow.

## Proposed offer

These prices are validation hypotheses, not existing commercial contracts.

| Offer | Proposed price | Intended scope |
|---|---:|---|
| Challenge demonstration | USD 0 | Included synthetic dataset, no real learner data and no service commitment. |
| 90-day validation pilot | USD 100 per school, or sponsor-funded | One school, controlled onboarding, agreed success measures and strict pre-pilot compliance gate. |
| School Core | USD 300 per school/year | Single school, up to five leadership users, dashboard/import workflow and a fair-use target of 500 Beacon requests/month. |
| Group or authority | Quoted after evidence | Multiple schools, central governance, integration and support; requires production database and tenant isolation. |

Zimbabwe commonly uses USD-denominated budgets for comparable technology purchases, but pricing and payment currency must be validated with participating schools and applicable financial requirements.

## Adoption pathway

1. **Synthetic demonstration:** judges and educators complete the fixed workflow without personal data.
2. **Discovery:** interview 5-10 school leaders about current review time, available fields, decision ownership and budget authority.
3. **Controlled validation:** after legal/security gates, run an 8-12 week pilot in 1-3 schools with a named data steward and weekly support.
4. **Evidence review:** compare data preparation effort, task completion, interpretation accuracy, interventions initiated and user trust against the pre-pilot baseline.
5. **Paid school release:** offer an annual school licence only if the workflow delivers measurable value and support cost is sustainable.
6. **Group expansion:** add multi-tenancy, integrations, central governance and a managed database before selling across school groups.

## Pilot success measures

- At least 90% of required upload rows pass validation after data-owner correction.
- A leader can identify the correct class/learner evidence and create a reviewed Decision Brief during a timed scenario.
- Zero unsupported numeric claims in the fixed live Beacon evaluation.
- Staff can distinguish a Beacon recommendation from a school decision in a short comprehension check.
- Every action has an owner, source evidence, review date and recorded outcome.
- No high-severity privacy/security incident and no unresolved pilot-blocking control.

The pilot must measure these outcomes; this document does not claim that they have already been achieved with real educators.

## Route to market

- Begin with direct founder-led demonstrations to secondary-school leadership and responsible authorities.
- Work through approved education innovation programmes, teacher/head associations and implementation partners where appropriate.
- Use a sponsor-funded pilot for resource-constrained schools only where governance and long-term ownership are clear.
- Publish a concise security/data-processing pack and fixed data template to reduce procurement and onboarding friction.
- Convert only after an end-of-pilot value review signed by the school and Veriq project lead.

## Retention and expansion logic

Annual renewal depends on recurring evidence reviews, not novelty of the chatbot. Useful expansion modules may include automated source integrations, intervention outcome tracking, district benchmarking with privacy-preserving aggregation and a locally hosted/open-weight explanation option. Each expansion requires its own validation and must not weaken the deterministic evidence boundary.

## Key commercial risks

- **Data preparation burden:** offer templates, validation feedback and steward training; do not hide poor source quality.
- **Low AI trust:** display evidence, limits and human ownership; measure comprehension rather than conversation volume.
- **Affordability:** cap model usage, share infrastructure across properly isolated tenants and validate sponsor/authority procurement.
- **Long procurement cycles:** start with a narrowly scoped, reversible validation agreement.
- **Support intensity:** standardise onboarding and include support effort in unit economics.
- **Regulatory cost:** treat legal, security and privacy work as a launch gate, not an optional later expense.
